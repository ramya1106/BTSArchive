About BTS

BTS, an acronym of Bangtan Sonyeondan or “Beyond the Scene,” is a Grammy-nominated South Korean group that has been capturing the hearts of millions of fans globally since its debut in June 2013. The members of BTS are RM, Jin, SUGA, j-hope, Jimin, V, and Jung Kook. Gaining recognition for their authentic and self-produced music, top-notch performances, and the way they interact with their fans, BTS has established themselves as “21st century Pop Icons” breaking countless world records. While imparting a positive influence through activities such as the LOVE MYSELF campaign and the UN ‘Speak Yourself’ speech, the band has mobilized millions of fans across the world (named ARMY), collected four No. 1 songs in a span of 9 months, performed multiple sold-out stadium shows across the world, and been named TIME’s Entertainer of the Year 2020. BTS has been nominated for Best Pop Duo/Group Performance for the 63rd Grammy Awards and recognized with numerous prestigious awards like the Billboard Music Awards, American Music Awards and MTV Video Music Awards.

EVOLUTION OF BTS

Previous Next

2013: Debut

BTS officially debuted on June 13, 2013, with the album "2 Cool 4 Skool" and its lead single "No More Dream. Despite modest initial success, BTS garnered attention for their energetic performances and sharp choreography. Their lyrics delved into the struggles of school-age youth, the pressures of society, and the journey towards self-discovery and acceptance. Their work extends to literature, philosophy, and a complex storyline, captivating global audiences with their versatile artistry and empowering messages.

Previous Next

2014: Rising Popularity

In 2014, BTS released their first mini-album "O!RUL8,2?" and continued to build their fanbase domestically and internationally. The single "Boy in Luv" marked their breakthrough in South Korea, earning them their first music show win. BTS embarked on their first concert tour, showcasing their powerful stage presence and solidifying their reputation as performers.

Previous Next

2015: International Recognition

BTS's third mini-album "The Most Beautiful Moment in Life, Pt. 1" introduced a more mature and introspective sound. The single "I Need U" achieved commercial success, winning numerous music awards and expanding BTS's global fanbase. They made their debut on Billboard's World Albums chart, signaling their growing popularity outside of Asia.

Previous Next

2016: Breakthrough

BTS released "The Most Beautiful Moment in Life: Young Forever," which concluded their "Hwa Yang Yeon Hwa" trilogy. The album's lead single "Fire" became a massive hit, further solidifying BTS's position in the K-pop industry. They made their first appearance at the Billboard Music Awards, winning the Top Social Artist award, a historic moment for a Korean act.

Previous Next

2017: Global Domination

BTS released the extended play "Love Yourself: Her," featuring the lead single "DNA," which peaked at number 67 on the Billboard Hot 100. Their popularity soared internationally, with sold-out concerts and appearances on prominent American talk shows. BTS became the first K-pop group to perform at the American Music Awards and the first Korean act to receive a certification from the Recording Industry Association of America (RIAA).

Previous Next

2018: Continued Success

BTS released the album "Love Yourself: Tear," which debuted at number one on the Billboard 200 chart, making them the first K-pop act to achieve this feat. They embarked on their "Love Yourself World Tour," selling out stadiums across Asia, North America, Europe, and South America. BTS addressed social issues and mental health in their music, resonating with a diverse audience worldwide.

Previous Next

2019: Record-breaking Achievements

BTS released the album "Map of the Soul: Persona," which debuted at number one on the Billboard 200 chart, making them the first group since The Beatles to achieve three number-one albums in less than a year. They continued to break records with their singles and albums, solidifying their status as global icons. BTS delivered a historic speech at the United Nations General Assembly, advocating for self-love and youth empowerment.

Previous Next

2020: Adaptation to the Pandemic

Due to the COVID-19 pandemic, BTS postponed their world tour and adapted to online concerts and virtual events to connect with fans. They released the album "Map of the Soul: 7," which received critical acclaim and further cemented their legacy in the music industry. BTS remained active on social media, engaging with fans and promoting positivity during challenging times.

Previous Next

2021: Cultural Impact

BTS released the English-language single "Dynamite," which debuted at number one on the Billboard Hot 100 chart, making them the first Korean act to achieve this milestone. They continued to advocate for social issues, including racial justice and mental health awareness, using their platform for positive change. BTS received numerous accolades, including Grammy nominations and recognition from prestigious institutions worldwide.

Previous Next

2022-2024: Further Growth and Exploration

BTS continued to push boundaries with their music, experimenting with different genres and collaborating with renowned artists. They expanded their endeavors beyond music, venturing into acting, philanthropy, and business ventures. BTS remained dedicated to their message of love, unity, and self-expression, inspiring millions of fans around the globe.

Movies/ Documentary

Burn the Stage (2018)

Where to watch: YouTube; myasiantv

Burn the Stage is an eight-part series that tells the story of BTS’ massive 300-day The Wings Tour, which took up most of 2017 for the members. Including interviews and show footage, Burn the Stage follows a pretty standard concert film format, giving insight into BTS’ first major world tour. The series would later be turned into a movie, also called Burn the Stage, which was released later in 2018.

Bring the Soul (2019)

Where to watch: Weverse; myasiantv

Similar to Burn the Stage, Bring the Soul is another glimpse into what BTS’ tour life is like. This six-part series follows the seven members of BTS during the Love Yourself World Tour, through the end of the tour’s third, European leg, which concluded in October 2019. It was also released in a more condensed movie format in Bring the Soul: The Movie.

Break the Silence (2020)

Where to watch: Weverse; myasiantv

BREAK THE SILENCE: THE MOVIE travels with BTS throughout the tour, exploring each band member behind the curtain. Offstage, we see another side of BTS. The seven members begin to candidly tell personal stories they have never voiced before. The tour took place across Los Angeles, Chicago, New York, São Paulo, London, Paris, Osaka, Shizuoka, Riyadh, and Seoul.

Suga: Road to D-Day (2023)

Where to watch: Weverse; Disney; myasiantv

The forthcoming documentary will follow Suga as he travels across the world in search of musical inspiration for his new album D-DAY, from Seoul to Tokyo, Las Vegas, and beyond. Viewers will see the singer at his most vulnerable throughout the documentary, as he discusses his writer's block with other musicians and digs deep into his darkest memories to craft lyrics for many of his current songs.

J-Hope in the Box (2023)

Where to watch: Weverse; myasiantv; Disney

A documentary that captures the album production process of J-Hope' first official solo album, "Jack In The Box" released in July last year and features his solo activities in a span of over 200 days, from working on the album's songs to listening parties with musicians and directors, the day before the album release, and the Lollapalooza performance in Chicago, USA.

Hope on the Street (2024)

Where to watch: Weverse; Prime Video; myasiantv

j-hope’s STREET DANCE REPORT. In 2024, j-hope of BTS begins a new journey in his 12th year, returning to his dancer roots. Accompanied by his former instructor, the popping champion Boogaloo Kin, he explores the streets of Osaka, Seoul, Paris, New York, and Gwangju, meeting inspiring street dancers along the way. Follow j-hope as he pursues a dream connecting his past, present, and future.

Notable Achievements

Rookie of the Year

BTS wins the 2013 “Rookie of the Year” at the Melon Music Awards on November 14, 2013, cementing their hard work during their debut year.

Billboard 200

BTS entered the Billboard 200 for the first time in 2015 with HYYH Pt.2 at #171. BTS was the first Korean Act to enter the Billboard 200 chart who were not under the two of the “Big 3” Korean agencies (SM and YG). No JYP artist entered the chart that time.

Honorary Tourism Ambassadors to the City of Seoul

On September 14, 2017, BTS was announced as Honorary Tourism Ambassadors to the City of Seoul at which they appeared in the video' 'BTS' Life in Seoul' to promote the city's major landmarks.

BTS Collaborates With UNICEF On Love Myself ENDviolence Campaign

On November 1, 2017, BTS and BigHit joined with the Korea Committee for UNICEF to sponsor the ‘#ENDviolence’ campaign aimed at ensuring children and teens in the world lead safe and healthy lives without the fear or violence.

BTS speech at the United Nations

On September 24, 2018, BTS participated in the UNICEF Generation Unlimited partnership event. RM became the first Korean singer to make a speech at the event delivering his own touching story to the world.

Top Social Award

BTS made history at the Billboard Music Awards as they became the first K-Pop artists to be nominated and to win the Top Social Artist award.

TIME Magazine's Most Influential People On The Internet

BTS has been named one of TIME Magazine's most influential people on the internet! To compile its unranked list, TIME looked at "their global impact on social media and their overall ability to drive news."

Forbes 30 Under 30

BTS earned a spot in the Entertainment & Sports section on the 2018 Forbes 30 Under 30 Asia list. The articles says: If you asked most Americans to name just one Korean performing artist in the last year, odds are high the answer would have been BTS...

Order of Cultural Merit

BTS received the Order of Cultural Merit from the Korean government. "The government decided to award all the members of BTS with the order of merit, as they have contributed to spreading Korean culture and language to other countries," Kim Eui-kyeom said. They became the youngest recipients of the award.

First Korean Group to Present at the GRAMMY Awards

BTS has made history as the first Korean group to present an award at the 61st GRAMMY Awards in Los Angeles, California.

Daesang Sweep At The MMA

BTS has made history at the 2019 Melon Music Awards! BTS took home a total of 8 awards, including all four Daesangs (grand prizes) with "Artist of the Year", "Record of the Year", "Album of the Year" and "Song of the Year".

All-Kill At MAMA

At the 2019 Mnet Asian Music Awards (MAMA) BTS became the first artist ever to win every Daesang (grand prize) at the ceremony. They took home 9 awards, with all 4 Daesangs. BTS made their way to the stage to collect “Album of the Year”, “World Icon of the Year”, “Song of the Year”, and “Artist of the Year”. They now hold the title of “most Daesang wins” in MAMA history!

Perform at the Grammy Awards

At the 62nd GRAMMY Awards on January 26, 2020, BTS became the first Korean group to perform on the GRAMMY stage. BTS performed Lil’ Nas X's smash hit “Old Town Road” along with Lil’ Nas X, Diplo, Nas, Mason Ramsey, and Billy Ray Cyrus. During the performance, RM rapped his verse from the “Seoul Town Road” remix.

BTS donates $1 million to Black Lives Matter

BTS stated their solidarity with #BlackLivesMatter this week, declaring “we stand together” against racial discrimination, the group and its Korean record label are making a further commitment: BTS and Big Hit Entertainment have donated $1 million to Black Lives Matter.
